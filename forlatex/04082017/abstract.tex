\title{Symmetry breaking operators of indefinite orthogonal groups $O(p,q)$}
\author{Alex Leontiev\\Graduate School of Mathematical Sciences, the University of Tokyo}
\date{August 04, 2017}
\begin{document}
\maketitle
In this talk I will present the joint work with T. Kobayashi.
%%on branching of infinitely-dimensional representations of non-compact groups. 
We classify the space $\Hom_{G'}(I(\lambda)\kern-0.1cm\mid_{G'},J(\nu))$ where $G\supset G'=O(p+1,q+1)\supset O(p,q+1)$ and $I(\lambda),J(\nu)(\lambda,\nu\in\C)$ are
degenerate spherical principal series representations of $G,G'$.
I will explain why this problem is important in representation theory, how it relates to other areas
and the techniques used in the proof.
%%This is inspired by the success in complete description 
%%of branching for compact groups. If $G$ is compact, $\pi$ is its irreducible representation (irrep, for short), and $G’$ is a subgroup, we have good
%%combinatorial results describing what $\pi$ looks like as sum of irreps of $G'$. On contrast, if groups are not compact and representations are infinite, 
%%the problem becomes much more involved. T. Kobayashi proposed a program to go further in branching 
%%problems when $\dim\pi=\infty$,
%%and one of the steps is to construct and classify symmetry breaking 
%%operators, for which the first complete results were published in Memoirs of AMS in 2015. 
%%This seems to be connected with various branches of mathematics, and we work
%%on a case $G=O(p,q)$.
